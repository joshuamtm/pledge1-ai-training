MEET the MOMENT

A Tale of Moths and Misguided Solutions

Last Tuesday at 6:14 AM...

I stood on a kitchen chair, wielding a vacuum cleaner against pantry moths that had transformed my oatmeal into a "lepidopteran discotheque."

My first instinct? Order a flamethrower from Amazon.

My son's response: "Dad, why don't we work on the root of the problem instead?"

The Lesson for AI Implementation

Like my moth situation, most organizations approach AI with:

  • Equal parts panic and denial
  • Misguided impulses to either ignore it or "burn it all down"
  • No systematic approach to the root challenges

That's why we need COMPAS - a framework for addressing root challenges, not symptoms.

C - Context: Understanding Your Landscape

Real Scenario: Priya's Panic

ED of statewide advocacy org: "My staff are using bots and I think I'm going to jail!"

The real context: Staff using AI without policies, training, or shared vocabulary

Context Gathering for Your Organization

  • What AI tools is your team already using (officially and unofficially)?
  • What problems are they trying to solve?
  • What are your compliance and security requirements?
  • What's your team's technical comfort level?

Context-Gathering Prompt:

"Help me map out all the ways my team might already be using AI tools, both officially and unofficially, and what problems they're trying to solve. Tell me all the information that might be helpful in understanding this and how I can best provide you with this information."

Advanced Prompting: The PTCF Method

Transform OK prompts into BEST prompts

P
Persona

Who should AI be?

T
Task

What needs doing?

C
Context

Background info

F
Format

How to present it

OK Prompt: "Write a grant report"

BEST PTCF Prompt:

"Act as a grant writing specialist for nonprofit organizations (P). Create a quarterly impact report for our youth mentorship program (T). We served 150 at-risk youth this quarter with 85% showing improved academic performance and 90% regular attendance (C). Format as a 2-page executive summary with bullet points for key metrics, narrative sections for success stories, and a data visualization section. Use accessible language at an 8th-grade reading level (F). Please ask me 3 clarifying questions before you begin."

Vibe Coding: Your Organizational Voice

What is a Vibe Card?

A set of rules that encode your organization's tone, style, and values into AI outputs

Sample Vibe Card for Social Impact Org

Tone: Warm, professional, hopeful
Voice: Active, people-first language<ишь> Reading Level: 8th-10th grade
Values: Emphasize community, equity, sustainability
Avoid: Jargon, passive voice, deficit-based language
Include: Success stories, data with context, calls to action

How to Create Your Vibe Card

  • Collect 3-5 samples of your best communications
  • Identify common patterns in tone and style
  • Note specific phrases and terms you use/avoid
  • Add to your Package instructions

Vibe Card Integration:

"Apply our Vibe Card: Use community-first, plain-English, people-first language. Maintain 8th-grade reading level. Emphasize hope and possibility while acknowledging challenges."

Essential Guardrails

Keeping AI Safe and Effective

✓
Human-in-the-Loop: All external communications reviewed by human
✓
Data Privacy: Never upload PII or sensitive donor information
✓
Spot Checks: Random review of AI outputs weekly
✓
Version Control: Date your Packages (v1.0, v2.0)
✓
Model Drift: Re-test every 6 months or after updates
Quick Compliance Checklist

☐ Is this use case allowed by our data policy?
☐ Have we removed all identifying information?
☐ Is a human reviewing before it goes live?
☐ Do we have a rollback plan if something goes wrong?

P & A: Performance and Assessment

Performance: Making it Real

Start small, measure impact, scale what works

  • Begin with one workflow this week
  • Track time saved and quality improvements
  • Share wins with your team
  • Remember: Sometimes the best solution isn't AI!

Assessment: Continuous Improvement

Monthly Review Questions

• Which workflows are saving the most time?
• What unexpected benefits have emerged?
• Where are we hitting friction?
• What needs to be adjusted or retired?
• How is the team feeling about AI tools?

Success Metrics

  • Efficiency: Hours saved per week
  • Quality: Error reduction, consistency improvement
  • Innovation: New capabilities enabled
  • Satisfaction: Team morale and reduced burnout
